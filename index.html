The Tale of Afghanistan: A Century of Turmoil

Founded in 1747 when Ahmad Shah Durrani unified the Pashtun tribes, Afghanistan has served as a buffer between the British and Russian Empires until it won independence from British control. The Afghanistan of today is a multiethnic and mostly tribal society. The population of the country consists of numerous ethnolinguistic groups: Pashtun, Tajik, Hazara, Uzbek, Aimaq, Turkmen, Baloch, Pashai, Nuristani Gujjar, Arab, Brahui, Qizilbash, Pamiri, Kyrgyz, Sadat and others.
